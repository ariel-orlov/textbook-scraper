# **Chapter 56**

# Conservation Biology and Global Change


#### Figure 56.1


Researchers discovered this colorful lizard, the psychedelic rock gecko (*Cnemaspis psychedelica*), during an expedition to the Greater Mekong region of Southeast Asia. In fact, between 2000 and 2010, more than  1,000 new species were discovered in this region alone. Unfortunately, the psychedelic rock gecko and many other newly discovered species’ survival is threatened by deforestation and other human activities.


![How can we protect the many species threatened by human activities?](images/img_1.jpg)


Ways to protect the diversity of life include:


Restore or preserve habitat that threatened species need to survive


End the overharvesting of species whose populations are in decline


Combat climate change and other types of human-caused, global environmental change


Establish regional networks of protected areas


Protect hot spots, relatively small areas with high biodiversity
